2. What happens when a required RAG source is unavailable or stale? Describe your fallback behavior in full detail:
If a required source is unavailable, stale, or returns conflicting data, the bot should not guess. It should:

Detect the missing source and classify the answer as incomplete.
Suppress any action-oriented or high-risk recommendation.
Tell the student which source could not be verified.
Provide only safe general guidance if possible.
Escalate to the correct human queue when the issue affects registration, financial standing, aid, or graduation.
Pass a structured handoff packet including student ID, requested intent, unavailable source, timestamp, and partial findings already confirmed from other systems.
Mark the case as “source unavailable / data freshness risk” for audit and follow-up.

Example fallback behavior:
“If registration_records is available but student_holds is stale, the bot may say, 'I can confirm you are not yet registered, but I cannot verify your active hold status right now. I don’t want to give you the wrong next step, so I’m routing this to advising support with your current registration context attached.’”

3. Which single metric, if it dropped below target, would signal a fundamental design problem — and what would you investigate first?
Escalation accuracy would be the most important single warning metric.

If escalation accuracy drops below target, it likely means the bot is misunderstanding intent, identifying the wrong blocker, or mapping cases to the wrong human team. That is a fundamental design problem because the whole system depends on correct routing across advising, registrar, bursar, financial aid, LMS, and help desk. The first things I would investigate are:

intent classification errors,
blocker-detection logic,
missing or stale source data,
confusing multi-intent student messages, and
whether the escalation rules are too broad or too narrow.

That focus directly matches the registrar workflow, finding that many “full class” complaints are actually caused by other blockers, and the AI opportunity file that stresses correct intake triage as a major value point.
